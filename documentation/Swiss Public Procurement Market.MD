# Swiss Public Procurement Market: Strategic Opportunity for SME Tender Platforms

Switzerland's **CHF 35-40 billion** annual public procurement market represents a compelling but fragmented opportunity. Despite SMEs comprising 99% of Swiss businesses, over 80% of ICT companies report no meaningful improvement in SME-friendliness since the 2021 legal reforms. The official SIMAP platform functions as a publication tool, not a discovery engine—creating a clear gap for intelligent tender matching services targeting underserved small and medium enterprises.

The market structure heavily favors cantonal and municipal procurement (80% of volume), where digitalization lags and expertise is thinnest. Current private platforms like Olmero and Devisio dominate construction but leave cross-sector SMEs with limited options beyond expensive pan-European aggregators or academic tools like IntelliProcure. The Swiss Trade Association's November 2025 formal complaint about "disproportionate administrative burden" signals growing political pressure to improve SME access—timing that favors new entrants with differentiated solutions.

---

## Market size reaches CHF 40 billion across three government tiers

The Swiss public procurement market operates across three distinct tiers, each with different access dynamics and competitive intensities.

**Federal procurement** accounts for approximately **CHF 7-8 billion** (20% of total volume), managed through centralized agencies including armasuisse, ASTRA, and BBL. Federal tenders appear consistently on SIMAP with standardized processes, making this tier the most accessible but also most competitive. The Federal Procurement Strategy 2021-2030 explicitly targets sustainability and innovation, with net-zero emissions mandated by 2040.

**Cantonal and municipal procurement** represents the lion's share at **CHF 28-32 billion** (80% of volume), but remains highly fragmented across 26 cantons and approximately 2,200 communes. This tier presents both the largest opportunity and greatest challenge: procurement practices vary significantly, with 5 cantons (including Geneva and Ticino) still operating under older legal frameworks. Published statistics for this tier remain scarce, representing a significant information asymmetry.

Construction work dominates the market at roughly **50% of total volume** (CHF 17-20 billion), reflecting Switzerland's substantial infrastructure renewal needs—approximately **CHF 19 billion annually** against an infrastructure replacement value of CHF 830 billion. IT services claim the second-largest share at **CHF 10-12 billion**, followed by transportation and healthcare.

| Government Level | Annual Volume | Share | Digitalization |
|------------------|---------------|-------|----------------|
| Federal | CHF 7-8 billion | ~20% | Advanced |
| Cantonal | CHF 15-18 billion | ~45% | Moderate |
| Municipal | CHF 12-15 billion | ~35% | Low |

SIMAP processes roughly **CHF 20 billion** through its platform annually, with approximately **3,000 procurement departments** and **50,000 registered vendors** active on the system. However, significant volume occurs below mandatory publication thresholds (CHF 150,000 for services, CHF 300,000 for construction), remaining invisible to current monitoring tools.

## Construction and IT dominate, but emerging verticals show growth potential

The sectoral distribution of Swiss public tenders reveals concentrated activity in traditional industries alongside emerging opportunities in sustainability and digital transformation.

**Construction and infrastructure** commands the largest share at approximately **40% of total tender volume**. The sector benefits from stable demand driven by SBB (Swiss Federal Railways), ASTRA (Federal Roads Office), and continuous municipal infrastructure projects. Quarterly civil engineering revenues reach CHF 2.1 billion, with building construction generating CHF 2.56 billion. This vertical is well-served by specialized platforms like Olmero and Devisio, creating high barriers for new entrants.

**IT services and software** represents the fastest-growing segment at roughly **25% of volume**, with federal ICT spending alone reaching CHF 1.3-2.4 billion annually. Critical insight: **approximately 50% of IT contracts are direct awards** compared to 10% in other sectors, suggesting significant procurement volume bypasses open tender procedures entirely. Top federal IT suppliers include Swisscom (CHF 129 million), SAP (CHF 45 million), and Atos (CHF 44 million).

**Underserved verticals** present the clearest opportunities for new platforms:

- **Healthcare and medical supplies**: Fragmented across 26 cantonal health systems with varying procurement sophistication, representing CHF 3-4 billion in annual volume
- **Sustainability and environmental services**: New since 2021 legal requirements mandate environmental criteria, but limited historical benchmarking exists
- **Professional services consulting**: Growing demand driven by digital transformation needs, particularly in smaller cantons lacking internal expertise
- **French and Italian language regions**: Many monitoring tools prioritize German content, leaving Romandie and Ticino procurement underserved

Cantonal and municipal procurement represents the most significant gap—comprising 80% of total volume but receiving the least platform attention. Small cantonal authorities frequently lack procurement expertise and would benefit most from intelligent matching and preparation assistance.

## SIMAP serves as publication infrastructure, not a discovery engine

The official Swiss tender platform underwent a complete redesign in July 2024, yet fundamental limitations for bidders persist.

**SIMAP (simap.ch)** functions as a joint electronic platform operated by the Confederation, all 26 cantons, and communes through an association structure. Key statistics reveal substantial scale: **20,000 daily users**, over **5,300 procurement organizations** since 2009, and **19,000+ registered bidders**. Access is free, with no subscription fees for searching or downloading tender documents.

The 2024 redesign introduced project-based organization (grouping publications, Q&A, and documents), role-specific interfaces, and online submission capability. However, SIMAP remains fundamentally an authority-facing publication tool with significant bidder-side limitations: no AI-powered matching, metadata-only search (cannot search within tender document content), limited market intelligence, and no historical win-rate analytics. The mobile app receives particularly poor reviews, with users citing "Windows 98 feel" and missing functionality compared to web.

**IntelliProcure (intelliprocure.ch)** emerged from Bern University of Applied Sciences as an intelligence layer built on SIMAP data. The platform indexes **2.1 million tender documents** from over 59,000 tenders, enabling full-text search within actual specifications—a major differentiator. Features include market analysis by CPV codes, supplier rankings, and daily email alerts matching keywords across document content. Pricing reportedly ranges CHF 200-800 annually, though not publicly disclosed. As an academic research tool, it prioritizes analytics over operational workflow support.

**TED (ted.europa.eu)** captures Swiss tenders above WTO thresholds (CHF 8.7 million for construction, CHF 230,000 for services) but misses most cantonal and municipal opportunities. Publishing 3,000+ notices daily across the EU with **€815 billion** in annual contract value, TED provides international exposure but lacks Swiss-specific depth.

| Platform | Type | Cost | Swiss Coverage | Full-Text Search | Bid Submission |
|----------|------|------|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| SIMAP | Official | Free | 100% | Metadata only | Yes |
| IntelliProcure | Academic | CHF 200-800/yr | 100% (via SIMAP) | Yes (2.1M docs) | No |
| TED | EU Official | Free | High-value only | Metadata only | No |

## Private platforms cluster around construction, leaving cross-sector SMEs underserved

The commercial tender monitoring landscape shows strong concentration in construction-specific solutions, with limited all-sector alternatives for Swiss SMEs.

**Tender Service Switzerland (tender-service.ch)** operates as the leading commercial aggregator, offering AI-powered TenderChat for document analysis, automated matching, market analytics, and tender forecasting. The platform provides subscription-based access with 7-day free trials. Strength lies in comprehensive AI tools and personal consultation; weakness includes premium pricing and primarily German interface.

**Olmero (olmero.ch)**, part of Hubexo Group, dominates construction with 20+ years of market presence, processing **5,000+ tenders annually** representing **CHF 8+ billion** in project volume. The platform specializes in digital tendering with CRB format support and maintains relationships with major clients including Implenia, HRS, and LIDL Schweiz. Free registration with premium modules creates a freemium model, but construction-only focus limits broader applicability.

**Devisio (devisio.ch)** serves as the second major construction platform with 1,800+ running tenders and 3,120 registered bidders. Key differentiator: free access for bidders combined with NPK (Swiss construction standards) compliance. Like Olmero, the construction-exclusive focus creates barriers for other sectors.

**DTAD (dtad.com)** represents the primary pan-European alternative with 450,000+ tenders annually and "Software Made in Germany" certification. Coverage spans 99% of European tender data with 40,000+ contracting authority profiles. Major clients include Bosch, Canon, and Siemens. Switzerland represents a secondary market focus.

**Gap analysis** reveals several underserved segments:

- No dominant cross-sector SME platform exists for Switzerland
- Current solutions lack intelligent SME-appropriate filtering by contract size, required references, and complexity level
- Consortium formation tools remain absent despite legal provisions encouraging bidder partnerships
- Below-threshold opportunity tracking is non-existent
- Win probability estimation based on historical data is unavailable
- French and Italian language regions receive inadequate coverage

| Competitor | Sector Focus | Pricing | Key Strength | Key Weakness |
|------------|-------------|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Tender Service | All sectors | Subscription | AI tools, analytics | Premium pricing |
| Olmero | Construction | Freemium | 20yr market presence | Sector limited |
| Devisio | Construction | Free for bidders | NPK compliance | Sector limited |
| DTAD | All sectors | Subscription | Pan-European scale | Switzerland secondary |
| IntelliProcure | Research | CHF 200-800/yr | Document search | Academic focus |

Adjacent services include bid-writing consultants (MR Schmid Consulting, PwC), legal specialists (CMS Switzerland, Public Sector Law), and training providers (procure.ch federal certificates, CIPS certifications via The Knowledge Academy from CHF 1,495).

## SME pain points create clear positioning opportunities

Research reveals systematic barriers that current solutions fail to address, creating differentiation opportunities for new market entrants.

**Administrative burden tops complaints.** The Swiss Trade Association formally demanded a regulatory burden study in November 2025, stating that "the effort to prepare an offer stands in no appropriate relation to the possible benefit" for SMEs. The Swico ICT survey confirms this perception: **90% of companies view quality competition as inadequate**, and over **80% see no real improvement** in SME-friendliness since the 2021 legal reforms.

**Resource constraints exclude smaller players.** Survey data indicates 58.7% find tender search effort too high compared to other sales channels, with only 9.5% considering public tender search effort lower than private-sector marketing. Reference requirements (often 5+ years of comparable projects) create a catch-22: SMEs need references to win contracts but need contracts to build references. High bonding and liquidity requirements further disadvantage startups.

**Language fragmentation creates invisible barriers.** Tenders publish in the issuing authority's language without standardized translation, meaning a Geneva company may miss relevant German-language opportunities from Zurich. SIMAP's newsletter publishes only in German and French, excluding Italian-speaking regions entirely.

**Discovery mechanisms fail SMEs.** Current platforms emphasize publication over intelligent matching. SIMAP offers basic keyword search without AI recommendations, no win probability estimation, and no filtering for SME-appropriate contract sizes. The 50% of IT contracts awarded directly never appear in open tender databases.

**Specific feature gaps identified:**

- No cross-lingual discovery spanning all Swiss language regions
- No SME-appropriate filtering by contract size, required references, or technical complexity  
- No consortium formation tools despite legal provisions allowing bidder partnerships
- No document preparation assistance or template libraries
- No win probability scoring based on historical data
- No below-threshold opportunity intelligence

Political momentum favors new entrants. The SGV's formal demands for burden reduction, combined with ongoing implementation of 2021 legal reforms explicitly targeting SME-friendliness, create favorable regulatory tailwinds. The 74.6% of SMEs who would search more frequently for public tenders if better tools existed represents quantifiable latent demand.

## Strategic positioning for market entry

The Swiss public procurement market offers a compelling but specific opportunity. Construction is well-served by incumbents; cross-sector SME solutions represent the clearest gap.

**Recommended positioning**: An intelligent tender matching platform differentiated by SME-appropriate filtering (excluding contracts requiring extensive references, excessive bonding, or multi-year track records), cross-lingual discovery across all Swiss regions, and affordable pricing scaled to company size. The CHF 200-800 annual pricing of IntelliProcure leaves room for a freemium model targeting micro and small enterprises.

**Priority features based on market gaps:**

- AI-powered matching with SME-appropriate parameters
- Win probability scoring using historical award data  
- Cross-lingual alerts (DE/FR/IT) for all Swiss language regions
- Consortium formation tools connecting complementary SMEs
- Simplified preparation assistance and document templates
- Below-threshold opportunity intelligence where accessible

**Target segments by priority**: Micro-enterprises (1-9 employees) needing simplicity and low cost; small enterprises (10-49 employees) requiring workflow tools and consortium support; professional services, healthcare, and sustainability consulting where sector-specific solutions remain limited.

The market offers approximately **50,000 registered SIMAP vendors** as the addressable universe, with CHF 35-40 billion in annual procurement volume and growing political pressure to improve SME access. Current paid solutions serve only a fraction of potential demand, and the leading academic tool (IntelliProcure) prioritizes research over operational workflows. For a new entrant focused on SME usability and intelligent matching, the Swiss market presents an attractive beachhead with expansion potential into adjacent European markets.
